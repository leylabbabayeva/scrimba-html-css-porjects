Top three places to visit in Baku

Ateshgah
Fire Temple

Ateshgah, also known as the Fire Temple of Baku, is an ancient religious site that has been a place of worship for Zoroastrians and later Hindu pilgrims. The name "Ateshgah" comes from the Persian word "Atash", meaning fire. The temple is famous for its eternal flames, which were once fueled by natural underground gas.

Icherisheher
Old City of Baku

Icherisheher, also known as Baku Old City or Inner City, is the historical heart of Baku and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is the oldest part of the city, surrounded by medieval fortress walls, and filled with narrow streets, ancient landmarks, and traditional Azerbaijani architecture.

Bibi-Heybat
Mosque

The Bibi-Heybat Mosque is one of the most significant and revered mosques in Baku, known for its beautiful architecture and historical significance. The mosque holds deep spiritual importance for the Azerbaijani people and is dedicated to Bibi-Heybat, the mother of the first Shia Imam, Ali.
